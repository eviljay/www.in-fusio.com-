In-Fusio working with Intel to develop a new
generation of games

On August 23, 2000 during the Intel Developer Forum, in San Jose, California, Intel will unveil its new Flash Data Integrator 3.0 software (FDI 3.0). This software manages code and data stored in flash memory. Intel has chosen to work with In-Fusio to demonstrate the performance of its FDI (Flash Data Integrator) technology with In-Fusio's gaming software.

"Intel is excited to be working with In-Fusio, one of the leaders in the cellular gaming world. We expect this effort to optimize the memory usage in cellular gaming applications as well as open the door for new In-Fusio games that benefit from FDI's Java and Java-like XIP capabilities," said Gary Forni, software manager of Intel's Flash Products Group.

Intel's new flash memory manager is particularly well adapted to mobile terminals, allowing service developers to get products to market more quickly. "More than any other service, games must be available in the shortest timescale possible. Thanks to Intel's technology, the deployment of a game will be much quicker and will therefore reduce the production costs. Moreover, the use of a FDI-managed flash memory provides large storage capacity and performance, opening up new horizons for the development of mobile phone games," emphasizes Giles Corbett, Managing Director of In-Fusio.

In fact, with FDI, games which require speed for processing and interactivity, such as Tetris or 3D labyrinth, will be easily adaptable to Intel's flash memory, whereas they would have been far too slow to run in WAP environment. For the player, there are also considerable advantages to the In-Fusio/Intel relationship. Now a player can manage his games' portfolio by adding, deleting or downloading them, say, from a PC directly into flash memory. The user will also have access to live multi-player games, where the participants can play simultaneously.

At theIntel Developer Forum, In-Fusio will demonstrate three new games using Intel flash memory and the Flash Data Integrator software: Pile-Up (a Tetris game), 3D Maze (a 3D labyrinth) and WataRaider (a platform game like Nintendo's Super Mario).

About In Fusio

Founded by Gilles Raymond in August 1998, In-Fusio pioneered the launch of games on mobile phones.

As both designer and developer, In-Fusio is the first company in the world to offer WAP and connected mobile games on mobile phones. Right from the start, In-Fusio stood out from conventional start-ups both in terms of customers and co-workers : 9 nationalities are already represented among the over-40 employees present on-site in Bordeaux working with 8 different mobile phones operators in 7 different countries world wide.
