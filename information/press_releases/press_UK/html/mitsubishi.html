Mitsubishi Electric Telecom Europe (METE) chooses In-Fusio’s ExEnÒ technology for downloading games for its Trium brand.

Bordeaux, June 14th 2001 Confirming its long-term collaboration with In-Fusio, the specialist in Mobile Connected Gaming, METE has just signed an agreement with the Bordeaux company for the integration of ExEnÒ , its downloadable gaming engine, into TRIUM cellular phones.

Following the development of both embedded games (TRIUM Luna and Laser) and interactive games (SMS), METE is continuing its collaboration with In-Fusio for the development of ExEnÒ downloadable games.

Created by In-Fusio, ExEn (Execution Engine) is the first virtual downloading engine entirely dedicated to games for cellular phones. This innovative technology allows players to download games, levels or characters to play on their cellular phone. The games are then run locally without the need for a continuous connection. The ExEnÒ technology is particularly well adapted to games containing animated graphics, sound, 3D and colors.

In the words of Gilles Raymond, In-Fusio's co-founder, President and CEO, "We are very excited about this new contract with TRIUM, one of the company’s longest-standing partners. Thanks to this agreement, In-Fusio will be able to prove once again to both manufacturers of cellular phones and operators that ExEnÒ is a standard, operational technology that can be integrated into all cellular phones."

According to Kouji Kodera, Marketing Director from Mitsubishi Electric Telecom Europe SA, "The market opportunity for mobile games is tremendous. Trium believes that mobile phones will play a significant role in this new entertainment field. Thanks to its past collaborations with In-Fusio, Trium has already proved that it could bring new revenue to Operators and strongly believes that the new ExEn platform will boost this trend."

About In-Fusio, the Mobile Game Connection

Pioneer and specialist in Connected Mobile Games, In-Fusio offers connected games and their full range of related services, such as organizing contests, managing the player community, hosting statistics for operators, etc. Today, In-Fusio has 300,000 regular players with 10,000,000 games played throughout the world.

In-Fusio’s offer is based on a portfolio of over 20 games and the development of its ExEnÒ technology and Gamezilla platform.

ExEnÒ is the very first technology totally dedicated to downloading games, while offering the very best video interface for real time animated graphics. Two of ExEn’s major assets include the fact that it is integrated into entry-level handsets and runs over today’s networks.

Gamezilla’s goal is to supply a single, coherent interface for the complete range of economic actors involved in the "Mobile Game Connection" (operators, manufacturers of cellular phones, game development studios, game and player animation services). www.in-fusio.com
