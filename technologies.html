In-Fusio breaks through mobile gaming limits with its unique ExEn technology. Coming in addition to other available and emerging technologies, In Fusio ensures the best gaming experience to the widest audience.

WAP – Text and simple graphic applications run off a central server, well suited to role playing and trivia. By early 2001 nearly all new handsets should support WAP.

IVR and SMS – Maximum appeal as this works on all handsets. The voice recognition interface is ideal for games such as Football Fans.

Embedded games — provide the very best video interface for real-time 3D graphics and are thus the obvious choice for arcade games. It requires specific handsets.

ExEn games – provide the very best video interface for real-time 3D graphics and therefore are the obvious choice for many games categories, such as arcade games and multi-player adventure games, when used in association with GPRS data channel. ExEn games are downloaded from the GameZilla platform and are executed on ExEn enabled handsets.

ExEn technology – ExEn is a unique virtual machine dedicated to games on mobile communication devices. Once integrated in the terminal, it allows games to be locally downloaded and executed. Game interactions with the server are then developed through available data channels: GPRS, SMS, GSM switched data. In opposition to general purpose Virtual Machines, ExEn provides to game applications a specific interface to ensure shorter games development lead time and best execution speed. Acknowledging its outstanding capabilities, several major handset manufacturers have already elected ExEn to be their mobile games script engine.

GameZilla platform – the In Fusio core body. This multi-service platform provides interface to all mobile gaming actors:

• Wireless operators (statistics, customer care, billing)
• Game studios developers (game development kit)
• Terminal manufacturers (ExEn)
• In Fusio games animators and media sales (contest management, advertising management),
• Mobile players (multiple access, single identification, loyalty program)
GameZilla is based on servers located closte to our customers and communicates through reserved bandwidth. GameZilla 1.0 is already in use, version 2.0 is scheduled for Q4 2000.
